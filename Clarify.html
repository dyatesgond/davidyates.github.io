Stage 1: Clarify the Goal, Challenge, Problem, or Opportunity


  • Start here when you are looking to improve, create, or solve something that is particularly perplexing.
  • Start here when you want to explore the facts, feelings and data around it.
  • Start here when you want to find the real problem to solve.

The following videos and valuable information about the CPS process can be found on the Create in a Flash section of Dr. Roger Firestien's website.



    A. IDENTIFY GOAL, WISH, CHALLENGE, OR PROBLEM

  1. Start with a goal, wish or challenge that begins with the phrase: I wish... or It would be great if...

    2. Diverge:

  2. If you are not quite clear on a goal, make a list of all of the things that you would like to create, invent, solve or improve.

    3. Converge:

  3. Select the goal, wish or challenge on which you have Ownership, Motivation and a need for Imagination. Avoid goals that can be completed successfully by following procedures, manuals, or accepted practices.

B. GATHER DATA

Diverge:
  1. What is a brief history of your goal, wish or challenge?
  2. What have you already thought of or tried? What might be your ideal goal?
  3. What have you already thought of or tried?

  4. What might be your ideal goal?

    5. Converge:

  5. Select the key data that reveals a new insight into the situation or that is important to consider throughout the remainder of the process.

C. CLARIFY THE GOAL, WISH, CHALLENGE, OR PROBLEM BY GENERATING CREATIVE QUESTIONS

Diverge:
  1. Generate 10-30 questions about your goal, wish or challenge.
  2. Phrase your questions beginning with: How to...?How might...? What might be all the ways to...?

  3. Try turning your key data into questions that redefine the goal, wish or challenge.

D. SELECT THE BEST PROBLEM

Converge:

  • Mark the Hits (New insights, promising directions, questions that nail it or feel good intuitively)
  • Group the related Hits together in clusters.
  • Label each cluster as a question beginning with How to..., What might be all the ways..., How might I...
