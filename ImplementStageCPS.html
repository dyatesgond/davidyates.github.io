Stage 4: Implementing An Action Plan to Give Your Solutions Legs

A. GENERATE ACTION STEPS

Diverge:

Using the following questions as a guide and a divergent thinking tool like Brainstorming, generate action steps you might take to implement your solution(s):

    • What might you do to make your solution easy to understand?
    • What might you do to demonstrate the advantages of your solution?
    • What might you do to gain acceptance for your solution?
    • What steps might you take to bring your solution to fruition?
    • Who might assist you in implementing your soluition?
    • Who might resist your solution and how might you win resisters over?
    • What additional resources might help you and how might you take advantage of them?
    • What attitudes, traditions, customs, or laws might assist you?
    • What might you do to measure and share your progress?

B. CREATE YOUR ACTION PLAN

Construct an action plan timeline divided into three periods with specific dates:

  • Short Term (actions that need to be competed immediately or at least before other action steps)

  • Medium Term (actions that should be completed after the Short Term ones but before the Long Term ones)

  • Long Term (actions that have no sense of urgency but still need to be completed eventually)

C. COMPLETE YOUR ACTION PLAN

Converge:

Use the Highlighting Tool to select the most promising of the action steps.

  1. Select the most promising of the action steps.
  2. Decide which action steps need to be completed in the short term and move them to that part of the timeline.
  3. Decide which action steps need to be completed in the medium term and move them to that part of the timeline.
  4. Decide which action steps need to be completed in the long term and move them to that part of the timeline.
  5. Assign each action step a target completion date.
  6. Assign someone to be responsible for completing each action step or, if a group is involved, indicating that the step has been completed.
  7. Assign a Report To for each action step.
