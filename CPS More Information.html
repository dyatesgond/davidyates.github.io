Does It Matter If You Are Trained in Creative Problem Solving?

For his dissertation research, Dr. Roger Firestien, Senior Buffalo State Professor compared creative productivity between CPS-trained groups and other groups not trained in creative problem solving.

  • The study involved 20 five-person groups who had approximately 12 hours of CPS training and 20 five-person groups who were not trained.
  • Researchers gave groups a business problem to solve and video recorded them at work.
  • As you an see from the first table to the right, groups trained in CPS showed significanty higher levels of participation, criticized ideas less, ad supported team members mmore.
  • They also smiled and laughed more, which is important because additional research has confirmed that positive group dynamics promotes creative problem solving.
  • As the second table indicates, the trained groups outperformed the untrained groups in generating highly-rated ideas.
